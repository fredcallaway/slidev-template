## The Availability Heuristic

**Classic findings about high availability of extreme negative events**

- **Definition**: We judge probability by how easily examples come to mind
- **Kahneman & Tversky's "K" study**: People think more words start with "K" than have "K" as 3rd letter (wrong - recall bias)
- **Famous names experiment**: Overestimate frequency of memorable items in lists

**Real-world examples:**
- **Plane crashes**: 1 in 11 million odds, but extensive media coverage makes flying seem riskier than driving (1 in 5,000)
- **Shark attacks**: ~5 deaths/year in US, but vivid media coverage inflates perceived risk
- **Terrorism**: Causes <1% of deaths globally, but dominates news and risk perception
